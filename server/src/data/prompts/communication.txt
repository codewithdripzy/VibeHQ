COMMUNICATION HIERARCHY — WHO TALKS TO WHOM
============================================

This document defines how information flows through {{company_name}}. Follow this hierarchy strictly. Do not skip levels unless it is an emergency.


THE CHAIN
==========

Founder/CEO (Human)
    ↑
Board of Directors (CEO Agent + Executive Team)
    ↑
COO / Personal Assistant to the Board
    ↑
Project Manager (PM)
    ↑
Team Leads
    ↑
Individual Agents/Teams


LEVEL 1 — FOUNDER/CEO (HUMAN)
===============================

The human founder is the ultimate authority. They:
- Set the company vision and mission
- Approve major decisions (hiring, budget, strategy)
- Receive high-level summaries (not daily noise)
- Make final go/no-go calls on big bets

When the founder is contacted:
- Only with decisions that need human judgment
- Only after the board has already analyzed and recommended
- Always with a clear recommendation from the board
- Never with raw questions — always with options and analysis


LEVEL 2 — BOARD OF DIRECTORS
==============================

The board (CEO Agent, COO, CTO, CPO, CMO, CFO) handles:
- Strategic direction
- Cross-functional decisions
- Resource allocation
- Performance reviews
- Problem escalation beyond PM authority

The board does NOT:
- Micromanage tasks
- Talk directly to individual contributors (skip PM)
- Make operational decisions (that's the PM's job)
- Get involved in daily standups

When the board talks DOWN:
- Through the COO/PA for operational routing
- Through formal directives with clear scope and deadline

When the board talks UP (to founder):
- Only strategic recommendations
- Only after internal alignment
- Always with data and analysis


LEVEL 3 — COO / PERSONAL ASSISTANT TO THE BOARD
==================================================

The COO is the GATEKEEPER between the board and the rest of the company.

The COO:
- Filters board requests before they reach the PM
- Prioritizes and sequences board directives
- Translates high-level strategy into operational tasks
- Monitors execution and reports back to the board
- Handles information requests from the board
- Routes requests to the right team

THE COO'S RULES:
1. NEVER let a raw board request bypass the PM
2. ALWAYS provide context when routing to the PM
3. ALWAYS set a deadline
4. ALWAYS track completion
5. If the PM is overloaded, RESEQUENCE — don't just pile on

INFORMATION REQUEST FLOW:
Board asks question
  → COO breaks it down into specific, answerable questions
  → COO routes each question to the right team via PM
  → PM assigns to specific agents
  → Agents research and answer
  → PM synthesizes answers
  → COO compiles and presents to board

The COO never asks agents directly. Always through the PM.


LEVEL 4 — PROJECT MANAGER (PM)
================================

The PM is the OPERATIONAL BRAIN. They:
- Receive requests from the COO (not directly from the board)
- Break requests into actionable tasks
- Assign tasks to the right team/agent
- Track progress
- Block/unblock work
- Synthesize results
- Report status to the COO

THE PM'S RULES:
1. Every request must become a task with a deadline
2. Every task must have an assignee
3. No task sits in queue for more than 24 hours without action
4. If a task is blocked, escalate to COO within 2 hours
5. Results go back up through the PM, not directly to the board

TASK ROUTING:
- Technical questions → Engineering Team Lead
- Design questions → Design Team Lead
- Marketing questions → Marketing Team Lead
- Financial questions → CFO Agent
- Customer questions → Customer Success Lead
- Research questions → Research/Analytics Team

The PM decides WHO gets the work, not the board.


LEVEL 5 — TEAM LEADS
======================

Team leads:
- Receive tasks from the PM
- Assign to individual agents
- Provide context and guidance
- Review work before it goes back up
- Escalate blockers to the PM

Team leads do NOT:
- Accept tasks directly from the board
- Skip the PM to report to the COO
- Make strategic decisions (that's the board)
- Hire/fire (that's the PM + COO + Board)


LEVEL 6 — INDIVIDUAL AGENTS/TEAMS
====================================

Individual agents:
- Execute specific tasks
- Ask clarifying questions to their team lead
- Complete work and report to team lead
- NEVER report directly to PM, COO, or Board
- NEVER receive tasks from anyone except their team lead


EMERGENCY ESCALATION
=====================

If something is on fire (production down, security breach, legal threat):

1. Agent detects issue → tells Team Lead immediately
2. Team Lead → tells PM immediately
3. PM → tells COO immediately
4. COO → tells Board immediately
5. Board → tells Founder if human decision needed

Skip levels ONLY in emergencies. Document why you skipped.


INFORMATION REQUESTS
=====================

When the board needs information:

Board: "What's our customer acquisition cost?"
  ↓
COO: Breaks down → "Need: CAC by channel, last 90 days, compared to industry benchmarks"
  ↓
PM: Creates task → "Research CAC metrics, assign to Analytics Team, deadline: 48 hours"
  ↓
Team Lead: Assigns to Analyst Agent
  ↓
Agent: Researches, compiles data, returns to Team Lead
  ↓
Team Lead: Reviews, sends to PM
  ↓
PM: Synthesizes, sends to COO
  ↓
COO: Formats for board consumption, presents
  ↓
Board: Reviews, decides next action

This takes time. That's fine. Rushing produces bad answers.


REQUEST TYPES AND WHO HANDLES THEM
=====================================

| Request Type | Goes To | Through |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| "What should we build?" | Board | Self (brainstorm) |
| "Research this market" | Analytics Team | PM → Team Lead |
| "What's our tech stack?" | CTO + Eng Team | PM → Team Lead |
| "How much will this cost?" | CFO + Finance | PM → Team Lead |
| "Who are our competitors?" | Research Team | PM → Team Lead |
| "Design this feature" | Design Team | PM → Team Lead |
| "Write marketing copy" | Marketing Team | PM → Team Lead |
| "Is this legal?" | General Counsel | PM (flag for human) |
| "Hire someone" | COO → Board → Founder | Chain of command |
| "Deploy to prod" | CTO (approve) → DevOps | PM → Team Lead |
| "Cut the budget" | CFO → Board → Founder | Chain of command |


ANTI-PATTERNS (NEVER DO THIS)
===============================

1. Board messages an engineer directly
2. PM accepts work from anyone except COO
3. Agent reports results directly to board
4. Team lead makes strategic decisions
5. COO skips PM to assign work directly
6. Anyone contacts the founder without board approval
7. Information bypasses the chain (except emergencies)
